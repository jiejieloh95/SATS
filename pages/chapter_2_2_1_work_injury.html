What is the difference between WICA and MOM Reportable?

WICA lets employees make claims for work related injuries or diseases, without taking legal actions.

For more information: http://www.mom.gov.sg/workplace-safety-and-health/work-injury-compensation

MOM Reportable is a work-related accident that is an unintended event that occurs in the course of work that leads to an injury or condition.

For more information: http://www.mom.gov.sg/workplace-safety-and-health/work-accident-reporting

As a manager, you need to report a work-related accident to MOM when the injured employee is given more than 3 days of medical leave, consecutive or otherwise, due to a single work-related accident.

You should count the calendar days of medical leave, even if the medical leave period extends over any public holiday, a rest day or non-working day. When filing the incident report, use the total number of days of medical leave granted as a result of the single work-related accident.

All work-related accidents must be reported to MOM within 10 days of accident or diagnosis, or from the 4th day of medical leave, whichever applies.

When you are evaluating whether an employee’s medical condition has resulted from a work-related incident, you need to take into consideration his job duties and working environment. Assess whether a work event or exposure to the employee’s working environment has caused or contributed to his medical condition or significantly aggravated an existing injury or medical condition.

The exceptions are:

• The medical condition involves signs or symptoms that surfaced at work but resulted solely from a non-work-related event or exposure.
• The medical condition resulted solely from voluntary participation in a non-work related activity.

You must obtain medical advice from the employee’s doctor-in-charge on whether there is a likelihood that his work duties and work environment had contributed to his medical condition or significantly aggravated an existing injury or illness.
