No. Eligibility is based on the citizenship of the employee’s child instead of the father’s citizenship. A working father is entitled to Government-Paid Paternity Leave (GPPL) for all births if you meet the following requirements:

• His child is a Singapore citizen born on or after 1 Jan 2017
• He is or had been lawfully married to the child's mother between conception and birth.
• He has served his employer for a continuous period of at least 3 months before the birth of his child.

P.S.: Effective 1 January 2017, male employees will be eligible for 2 week of GPPL within 16 weeks from the birth of the child.
